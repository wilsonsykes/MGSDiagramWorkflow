Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Regional Sales Manager checks sales history for upcoming re-orders. Review next week's route schedule for upselling opportunities. (S1.1.1)
  2. Sales Assistant 1 calls client for re-orders, or find new client orders. (S1.1.2)
  3. Sales Assistant 1 creates a draft Sales Order for each order. (S1.1.3)
  4. Regional Sales Manager forwards the Sales Order to Credit and Collection Analyst to see for past dues. (S1.2.1)
  5. If the customer has past dues, flag the upcoming order. If past dues are within a passable / supervisor-approved range, approve the upcoming order. If there are no past dues, approve the upcoming order directly. (S1.2.2)
  6. Order Fulfilment: Operations Supervisor makes Pick List, and Delivery Receipt. See Operations Commercial Procedure (S1.3.1)
  7. Logistics Officer updates the synced Operations Calendar in Monday.com for delivery schedules. (S.1.3.2)
  8. Sales Assistant 1 assigns and logs notes, activities, and tags for each Sales Order. (S.1.3.3)
  9. Regional Sales Manager Evaluate Sales Target vs Actual Sales. (S1.3.4)
  10. Sales Assistant 1 records sales-fulfilment concerns/issues and log Accounting-related notes. (S1.3.5)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Sales Order Control Numbers are non-editable once assigned - a wrong entry is corrected with a new document, never by editing the control number.
  • The upsell review happens before the order is finalized, as a scheduled step in the flow, not an optional add-on afterward.

DEALER
Find Client | Serve | Schedule | Repeat Order
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Sales Assistant 2 coordinates the dealers’ Plant Visit dates in advance. (S3.1.1.1)
  2. Sales Assistant 2 sends schedules and updates through the Viber Dealer Group. (S3.1.1.2)
  3. Security guard verifies dealer identity and authorization; only confirmed appointments are accommodated. (S3.1.2.1)
  4. Dealer brings empty cylinders. See Operations – Dealer procedures. (S3.1.2.2)
  5. XXX Warehouse Staff applies dealer pricing, adjusted within the per-dealer buffer. (S3.1.3.1)
  6. Operations Supervisor sets payment terms - installment with Post-Dated Checks (PDCs), COD, or a pre-approved Promissory Note. (S3.1.3.2)
  7. Accounting Sales Encoder records dealer sales and issues Charge Invoices. (S3.1.3.3)
  8. Sales Assistant 2 maintains dealer records, runs call-outs and follow-ups, and handles pricing disputes/adjustments. (S3.1.4.1)
  9. Sales Assistant 2 handles cylinder swapping, marketing support with Sir CDV, and pricing-adjustment requests with Regional Sales Manager. (S3.1.4.2)
  10. New Dealer Accreditation: applicant proposes a business location with no existing dealer coverage. (S3.1.5.1)
  11. Once Sir RY approves, SA2 secures DTI Registration, Business Permit, and BIR Registration. (S3.1.5.2)
  12. Submit the dealership application and secure the Supplier's Certificate. (S3.1.5.3)
  13. Complete DOE requirements (Annex G and Annex V5) and execute a Special Power of Attorney (SPA). (S3.1.5.4)
  14. Pay processing fees and submit documents to the Makati Office; coordinate with the DOE liaison for processing. (S3.1.5.5)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Two groups of dealers – Truck Pickup and Deliver and Plant Pickup
  • Only confirmed, scheduled dealer appointments are accommodated - see plant-refilling rule in Operations > Dealer.
  • New dealers must maintain a minimum of 50 LPG cylinders; MGC may process DOE/permit requirements on the applicant's behalf, or the applicant may self-file.
  • Cylinder swapping, marketing support, and new pricing adjustments all route through the same five-step approval chain ending in Plant Operations Officer sign-off - no skipping to a later approver.
  • Dealer pricing is adjusted only within the per-dealer price discount-distance buffer defined in the MGC Pricing Matrix.

SEMI-DEALER
Find Client | Serve | Schedule | Repeat Order
Partially Confirmed ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Solicit and plan orders against daily and monthly Semi-dealer sales targets. (S4.1.1.1)
  2. Call clients for re-orders and find new client orders; review sales history and next week's route schedule. (S4.1.1.2)
  3. semi-dealer clients buy smaller quantities, mainly 11kg and 2.5kg, on a Semi-dealer scale. (S4.1.1.3)
  4. Apply pricing: offer SRP first, then the Minimum Price List (MPL) with volume discounts (3-5, 6-10 units) and distance-zone pricing (0-30km, 30.1-40km, 41-60km). (S4.1.2.1)
  5. Semi-dealer customers may submit a portal order (with discount); Sales Assistant 2 approves against schedule and payment terms, Regional Sales Manager validates. (S4.1.2.2)
  6. Fulfil: Sales Assistant 2 lines up confirmed Sales Orders in Monday.com; Logistics Officer reviews the lined-up orders and routes. (S4.1.3.1)
  7. Delivery driver carries cash invoice for COD. Driver brings additional buffer cylinders for extra sales. (S4.1.3.2)
  8. Upon return, delivery driver returns issued cash invoices and cash to OIC-Accounting. (S4.1.3.3)
  9. Post-sales: log concerns, evaluate sales target vs actual, and sync with the Operations calendar. (S4.1.3.4)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Semi-dealer pricing always follows SRP first, then falls to the Minimum Price List with volume/zone discounts - MPL is the floor, not the opening offer.
  • Cash Invoice Control Numbers are non-editable, the same rule as Commercial.
  • Semi-dealer is COD/cash-invoice by default; the delivery team physically carries the cash invoice and reconciles it same-day with OIC-Accounting.

OVERALL
Reports | Monitoring | Compliance | Write-Offs
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. IT Manager prints and verifies multi-period Sales Performance Reports (SPR) bi-weekly or monthly. (S51.1.1)
  2. SPR fields include sales per kg, total sales, net sales, cost of sales, and Gross Profit Margin per kg and total. (S51.1.2)
  3. Pricing Matrix Process: Regional Sales Manager sets up the MGC LPG Pricing Matrix for decision; Sir RY decides on the matrix for the period, 1-3 times per month in a General Meeting. (S5.1.2.1)
  4. Pricing Matrix covers Sales Price, MPL, and Total Cost (Acquisition Cost + Hauling) per kg, by avenue - Commercial through Semi-dealer and semi-dealers - plus MPL volume discounts by radius. (S5.1.2.2)
  5. Regional Sales Manager and IT Manager updates competitor end-user pricing prior to General Meeting (SRP comparison). (S5.1.2.3)
  6. Price Cascade Process: Post-meeting, Regional Sales Manager emails the updated changes; IT Manager records the audit log for the change. (S5.1.2.4)
  7. IT Manager informs CODO, contact centers, marketing, and cashiers. Cascading is channel-owned: Regional Sales Manager and Sales Assistant1 - Commercial/Bulk clients, Sales Assistant2 - Semi-dealer clients; AVP Sales - Makati accounts (Jen); IT Manager - CODO/audit and contact centers/marketing/cashiers. (S5.1.2.5)
  8. Sir RY sets the Sales Quota per client per period; AVP Sales prints and uses it as the working target; Sales Assistant updates it daily/weekly. (S5.1.2.6)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Price changes are decided in the General Meeting by Sir RY - no ad hoc price change cascades without going through this meeting.
  • Every price change is audit-logged by the IT Manager, before it cascades to any client-facing channel.
  • Cascade responsibility is channel-specific, does not overlap.
