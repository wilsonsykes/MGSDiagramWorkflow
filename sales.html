COMMERCIAL

Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Call client for re-orders, or find new client orders. (S1A1.1)
  2. System auto-reminds the client and Sales Manager ahead of an impending order based on forecast. (S1A1.2)
  3. Create a draft Sales Order, Pick List, and Delivery Receipt for each order. (S1A1.3)
  4. Check Sales History for upcoming re-orders. (S1A1.4)
  5. Review current and upcoming inventory levels for upselling opportunities. (S1A1.5)
  6. Review next week's route schedule for upselling opportunities. (S1A1.6)
  7. Confirm the Sales Order and Invoice; auto-assign a permanent, non-editable Sales Order Control No. (S1A2.1-S1A2.3)
  8. Update the synced Operations Calendar for current deliveries. (S1A2.4)
  9. Assign and log notes, activities, and tags for each Sales Order. (S1A2.5)
  10. Post-sales: evaluate Sales Target vs Actual Sales; sync with the Operations Calendar and actual delivery. (S1A3.1-S1A3.2)
  11. Real-time computation of gains/losses per trip, per driver/helper and back-office team. (S1A3.3)
  12. Record sales-fulfilment concerns/issues and log Accounting-related notes. (S1A3.4-S1A3.5)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Sales Order Control Numbers are permanent and non-editable once assigned - a wrong entry is corrected with a new document, never by editing the control number.
  • The upsell review (inventory levels, route schedule) happens before the order is finalized, as a scheduled step in the flow, not an optional add-on afterward.
  • Gains/loss variance per trip is computed against the Operations Calendar in real time; unresolved fulfilment concerns are logged, never left unrecorded.

DEALER
Find Client | Serve | Schedule | Repeat Order
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Sales Assistant coordinates the dealer's Plant Visit Calendar in advance, contacting dealers roughly three times a week.
  2. Claire sends schedules and updates through the Viber Dealer Group.
  3. Dealer identity and authorization are verified; only confirmed appointments are accommodated.
  4. Confirm the dealer maintains at least 50 cylinders on hand; dealers typically buy in larger quantities.
  5. Dealer advance quantity requests/orders are disseminated to multiple staff; the dealer database is kept current. (S3A1.3-S3A1.4)
  6. Dealer brings empty cylinders for refilling or swapping (typically 8 tons).
  7. Apply dealer pricing from the MGC Pricing Matrix, adjusted within the per-dealer buffer.
  8. Set payment terms - installment with Post-Dated Checks (PDCs), COD, or a Promissory Note if no PDC is available.
  9. Produce the digital Sales Order and its counterpart Invoice, editable before confirmation; produce LUT, EES and other documents for printing. (S3A2.1-S3A2.2)
  10. Confirm the order - triggers instant Sales/Inventory/Invoice movement on a shared, multi-user dashboard. (S3A2.3-S3A2.4)
  11. Fulfil: Mel schedules the plant visit/delivery and coordinates truck availability.
  12. Post-sales: Claire maintains dealer records, runs call-outs and follow-ups, and handles pricing disputes/adjustments. (S3A3)
  13. Cylinder swapping, marketing support, and pricing-adjustment requests route through: Sales Assistant -> Sir RY -> Warehouse and Quality Supervisor -> Senior Supervisor -> Operations Officer. (S3A3.1-S3A3.5)
  14. New Dealer Accreditation: applicant proposes a business location with no existing dealer coverage.
  15. Once approved, secure DTI Registration, Business Permit, and BIR Registration.
  16. Submit the dealership application and secure the Supplier's Certificate.
  17. Complete DOE requirements (Annex G and Annex V5) and execute a Special Power of Attorney (SPA).
  18. Pay processing fees and submit documents to the Makati Office; coordinate with the DOE liaison for processing.

2Operational Guidelines

  • Only confirmed, scheduled dealer appointments are accommodated - mirrors the plant-refilling rule in Operations > Dealer.
  • New dealers must maintain a minimum of 50 LPG cylinders; MGC may process DOE/permit requirements on the applicant's behalf, or the applicant may self-file.
  • Cylinder swapping, marketing support, and new pricing adjustments all route through the same five-step approval chain ending in Operations Officer sign-off - no skipping to a later approver.
  • Dealer pricing is adjusted only within the per-dealer buffer defined in the MGC Pricing Matrix; adjustments outside the buffer require the same escalation used for a swap request.

SEMI-DEALER
Find Client | Serve | Schedule | Repeat Order
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Solicit and plan orders against daily and monthly Semi-dealer sales targets.
  2. Call clients for re-orders and find new client orders; review sales history and next week's route schedule.
  3. Take the order: confirm the Sales Order and assign a permanent, non-editable Sales Order Control No.
  4. Semi-dealer and semi-dealer clients buy smaller quantities, mainly 11kg and 2.5kg, on a Semi-dealer scale.
  5. Apply pricing: offer SRP first, then the Minimum Price List (MPL) with volume discounts (3-5, 6-10 units) and distance-zone pricing (0-30km, 30.1-40km, 41-60km).
  6. Semi-dealer customers may submit a portal order (with discount); Sales Assistant approves against schedule and payment terms, Sales Manager validates. (S4A1)
  7. Set payment: mostly COD/cash invoice; the delivery team carries the cash invoice.
  8. Fulfil: Sales Assistant lines up confirmed Sales Orders; Purchaser/Dispatcher reviews the lined-up orders and routes. (S4A2)
  9. Post-sales: log concerns, evaluate sales target vs actual, and sync with the Operations calendar.
  10. Compute gains/losses per trip per driver/helper; record customer/location/category sales contribution.

2Operational Guidelines

  • Semi-dealer pricing always follows SRP first, then falls to the Minimum Price List with volume/zone discounts - MPL is the floor, not the opening offer.
  • Sales Order Control Numbers are permanent and non-editable, the same rule as Commercial.
  • Semi-dealer is COD/cash-invoice by default; the delivery team physically carries the cash invoice and reconciles it same-day with Accounting > Semi-dealer.

OVERALL
Reports | Monitoring | Compliance | Write-Offs
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Print and verify multi-period Sales Performance Reports (daily/weekly/monthly, per sales avenue). (S5A1)
  2. Report covers invoice status and incentive gains/loss; issue Invoice-linked Credit Notes for Commercial clients and the delivery team. (S5A1.3)
  3. IT Head (Jabert) prints the Sales Performance Report (SPR) bi-weekly or monthly depending on inventory volume; fields include sales per kg, total sales, net sales, cost of sales, and Gross Profit Margin per kg and total.
  4. Regional Sales Manager (Mel) sets up the MGC LPG Pricing Matrix for decision; Sir RY decides on the matrix for the period, 1-3 times per month in a General Meeting. (S5A2)
  5. Pricing Matrix covers Sales Price, MPL, and Total Cost (Acquisition Cost + Hauling) per kg, by avenue - Commercial through Semi-dealer and semi-dealers - plus MPL volume discounts by radius.
  6. Regional Sales Manager and IT Head update competitor end-user pricing (SRP comparison).
  7. Price Cascade Process: Regional Sales Manager emails the updated changes; IT Head records the audit log for the change.
  8. IT Head informs CODO, contact centers, marketing, and cashiers. Cascade is channel-owned: Regional Sales Manager and Sales Assistant1 - Commercial/Bulk clients (Mel, Lou); Sales Assistant2 - Semi-dealer clients (Claire); AVP Sales - Makati accounts (Jen); Jabert - CODO/audit and contact centers/marketing/cashiers.
  9. Sir RY sets the Sales Quota per client per period; AVP Sales prints and uses it as the working target; Sales Assistant updates it daily/weekly. (S5A3)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Price changes are decided only in the General Meeting (1-3x per month) by Sir RY - no ad hoc price change cascades without going through this meeting.
  • Every price change is audit-logged by the IT Head before it cascades to any client-facing channel.
  • Cascade responsibility is channel-specific and does not overlap: Mel/Lou (Commercial/Bulk), Claire (Semi-dealer), Jen (Makati), Jabert (CODO/contact centers/marketing/cashiers) - a price update is not company-wide live until all channels confirm cascade.
