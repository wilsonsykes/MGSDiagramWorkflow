COMMERCIAL
Process Order | Delivery | Return to Plant
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Receive the approved Sales Order; client orders 22kg or 50kg cylinder variants. (O1.1.1)
  2. Logistics Officer schedules the delivery - vehicle, capacity, route. (O1.2.1)
  3. XXX generates the Picklist and YYY picks the LPG/products. (O1.1.2)
  4. XXX generates the Delivery Receipt (DR) - 2 DRs and 2 Credit Notes per delivery. (O1.1.3)
  5. XXX submits the DR to Accounting to post General Ledger and Inventory entries. (O1.1.4.1)
  6. Helper collects the Delivery Receipts from XXX in Warehouse (may cover multiple clients on one route). (O1.1.4.2)
  7. Driver and Helper proceed to client locations. (O1.1.4.3)
  8. For pickup / no-delivery orders, Warehouse gives the DR to the customer for signature instead. (O1.1.4.4)
  9. In client site, Helper weighs and records the cylinders with the client (Weigh In), then carries them to area. (O1.1.5.1)
  10. Client representative signs the DR, receives the new cylinders, and returns the empty cylinders. (O1.1.5.2)
  11. Helper weighs the returned cylinders (Weigh Out) in the present of Client Rep, classifies the 1-3kg residue threshold; round up or down. (O1.1.5.3)
  12. Helper provides Credit Notes to the Client, with client representative’s countersignature, recording cylinder serial number and weight residue. (O1.1.5.4)
  13. Driver and Helper return to the plant and weighs the empty cylinders, checks the 1-3kg residue threshold again. (O1.1.6.1)
  14. Driver submits the signed DR, signed Credit Notes and other records to YYY Accounting. (O1.1.6.2)
  15. YYY in Accounting reconciles the General Ledger against the signed Delivery Receipt and Credit Notes. (O1.1.7.1)
  16. Compute Commercial Gains - compare weigh-in vs weigh-out records from client site and plant. (O1.1.7.2)
  17. Industrial Engineer and YYY record results in Google Sheets and ACCPAC. (O1.1.7.3)
  18. Industrial Engineer submits Computation Sheet for CEO’s approval (O1.1.7.4)
  19. OIC-Accounting distribute incentives via Payroll to the involved members. (O1.1.7.5)

2Operational Guidelines

  • The Commercial residue classification threshold is fixed between 1-3kg; deviations are escalated to the Warehouse Supervisor, not decided ad hoc by the driver.
  • Two DRs and two Credit Notes are mandatory per delivery (client copy + company copy); a delivery without a matching document pair is not closed in the GL.
  • No cylinder leaves the warehouse without a Picklist form; no GL/Inventory posting is made without a Warehouse-submitted DR.
  • Incentive Gains are computed only from matched weigh-in/weigh-out pairs - unmatched records are held for investigation, never estimated.

4Current vs. Future Workflow

Current — Traditional / Manual
  • Paper DR/CR forms filled by hand at warehouse and client site.
  • GL reconciled manually against physical DR/CR copies.
  • Gains keyed manually - once to Google Sheets, formula and total reviews needed
  • Residue judged by the driver/helper at point of delivery.
Future — Target / Automated
  • Odoo Sales Order auto-generates Sales Quotation, Picklist, DR. Add the Invoice, Client’s Credit Notes and Team’s Incentive (also under Credit Notes) and GL, in one continuous workflow.

  • Gains keyed once in Odoo Sales Order. Auto-computes gains for summary, and team incentive.
  • Single-entry GL posting, removing the GSheet/ACCPAC duplicate entry.

BULK
Process Order | Delivery | Return to Plant
Partially Confirmed ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Sales Rep receives the bulk Sales Order; the order must fill the 8-ton truck capacity. (O2.1.1.1)
  2. Logistics Officer assign the lorry/trailer truck and load from the Storage Tank (stock transfer, tank to truck - no cylinders involved). (O2.1.2.1)
  3. XXX Weigh the loaded truck at the plant truck scale (weigh-out). (O2.1.2.2)
  4. XXX Generate loading documents: Stock Transfer Form, Truck Scale slip, and Delivery Receipt - issued per lorry load. (O2.1.2.3)
  5. Delivery and Helper deliver the bulk LPG to the industrial or commercial client site. (O2.1.3.1)
  6. At the client site, helper measures the volume delivered using meter/gauge. (O2.1.3.2)
  7. Client receives the bulk LPG and signs the Delivery Receipt. (O2.1.3.3)
  8. Driver, helper and truck return to the plant and truck is weighed again (weigh-in). (O2.1.3.4)
  9. Driver returns the signed DR and xxx documents to Operations Supervisor. (O2.1.3.5)
  10. Industrial Engineer computes Bulk Gains per truck - compare weigh-out vs weigh-in; should read the higher figure. (O2.1.4.1)
  11. Industrial Engineer records residue in the Commercial & Bulk LPG Residue Summary Gsheet, per truck. (O2.1.4.2)
  12. Operations Supervisor submits documents to XXX (Accounting); record in Google Sheets and ACCPAC. (O2.1.4.3)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Bulk orders must fill the 8-ton truck capacity before dispatch is scheduled; a partial load requires Plant Operations Officer approval as a documented exception.
  • Bulk order uses truck-scale (plant) + meter/pressure-gauge (client) measurement and truck weigh-in/out; both readings are logged before the trip is closed.
  • Per-truck gain is computed only once both weigh-out and weigh-in are on file - a truck missing either reading stays open, it is not estimated.

DEALER
Process Order | Plant Refilling & Swapping | Invoice
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Dealer client orders 2.5kg, 11kg or 22kg cylinder variants with Sales Assistant 2, and Plant visit scheduled in advance; only confirmed appointments are accommodated. (O3.1.1.1)
  2. Sales Assistant 2 creates a Sales Order Form noting the delivery/pickup date. (O3.1.1.2)
  3. Sales Assistant 2 forwards the orders to Credit/Collection Analyst to check for AR dues. (O3.1.1.3)
  4. If with past dues, don’t serve. If with past dues but with CEO approval, good to serve. If without past dues good to serve. (O3.1.1.4)
  5. Dealer arrives with empty cylinders; guard records the vehicle entry and confirms tank classification. (O3.1.2.1.1)
  6. Main Dealers arrive with empty cylinders to fill up 8 tons worth of cylinders. (O3.1.2.1.2)
  7. Checker records cylinder quantities and issues Entry/Exit Slip (EES). (O3.1.2.2)
  8. Cylinders are refilled at the refilling area. (O3.1.2.3)
  9. Checker encodes the transaction in AppSheet - this is the Refilling Report and the basis of the GRR (General Refilling Report). (O3.1.2.4)
  10. Checker issues Loading/Unloading Truck (LUT) to the Driver. (O3.1.2.5)
  11. Security verifies the LUT; cylinder quantity must match with LUT before release. (O3.1.2.6)
  12. If quantity does not match, Security flags and informs Plant Operations Officer or Senior Plant Supervisor. (O3.1.2.7)
  13. Plant Operations Officer reviews and confirms the transactions and records in XXX. (O3.1.3.1)
  14. Accounting verifies via AppSheet against the Cash Invoice or Credit Invoice. (O3.1.3.2)
  15. (covers pickup-with-terms, pickup-with-payment, and delivery-with-terms variants (O3.1.3.3)
  16. Cylinder Swapping: dealer messages Sales Assistant 2 for a swap request. (O3.1.4.1)
  17. Sales Assistant 2 verifies the customer database provided by the Dealer. Only approved allied brands allowed. O3.1.4.2)
  18. Sir RY reviews and approves before the swap proceeds. (O3.1.4.3)
  19. Warehouse and Quality Supervisor handles the physical swap and keeps records. (O3.1.4.4)
  20. XXX keeps a monthly record of swapped cylinders quantity, treatment and disposition. (O3.1.4.5)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Only confirmed, scheduled appointments are accommodated at the plant - walk-in dealer refills are not processed without a prior booking.
  • AppSheet entry and the official receipt must match in quantity before a transaction is released; a mismatch is held at the gate.
  • Cylinder swapping is restricted to approved allied brands only and requires Sir RY's approval.
  • Dealers must provide their own customer database for swap request.

OVERALL
Purchase Order | Stock Transfer | Refill | Floor Stock
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Logistics Officer forwards the Purchase Order to the Refinery Supplier and schedules the inbound Lorry Truck delivery. (C5.1.1.1)
  2. Plant Operations Officer receives the Lorry Trucks, collects the Delivery Receipt and Invoice from the Driver, fills out the Receiving Report (RR) and unloads contents to the Storage Tank. (C5.1.1.2)
  3. Plant Operations Officer forwards XXX documents to Logistics Officer. (C5.1.1.3)
  4. Logistics Officer records purchases in ACCPAC. (C5.1.1.4)
  5. Logistics Officer uses prices of past orders for pricing matrix recommendations. (C5.1.1.5)
  6. Operations Supervisor measures the content volume using a gauge and writes the reading. (C5.1.2.1)
  7. Operations Supervisor logs further daily readings as a separate entry. (C5.1.2.2)
  8. Stock Transfer: Operations Supervisor oversees cylinder refill from the Storage Tank following FIFO. (C5.1.3.1)
  9. Operations Supervisor tracks refill using AppSheet. (C5.1.3.2)
  10. Operations Supervisor tracks residue of two tanks in two categories, Regular and WIWO, both affecting inventory and billing. (C5.1.3.3)
  11. Pull-out schedules and dates at client sites are tracked against a company Pull-out Calendar. (C5.1.4.1)
  12. Logistics Officer purchases cylinders annually with individual serial numbers. (C5.1.4.2)
  13. Warehouse and Quality Supervisor repaints and counts cylinders, maintains cylinder quality record. (C5.1.4.3)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Storage Tank stock transfer to cylinders must follow FIFO; every batch is filled out in FIFO sequence with Plant Operations Officer approval.
  • Any quantity variance discovered after the Receiving Report is logged as a separate exception entry, not folded silently into the original RR figure.
  • Cylinder serials and batch numbers are tracked for their full lifespan (purchase through repaint/retest cycles); a cylinder without a traceable batch number does not re-enter circulation.
