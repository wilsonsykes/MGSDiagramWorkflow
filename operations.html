COMMERCIAL
Process Order | Delivery | Return to Plant
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Receive the approved Sales Order; client orders 22kg or 50kg cylinder variants. (C1.1.1)
  2. Delivery Head schedules the delivery - vehicle, capacity, route. (C1.2.1)
  3. Warehouse generates the Picklist and picks the LPG/products. (C1.1.2)
  4. Warehouse generates the Delivery Receipt - 2 DRs and 2 Credit Notes per delivery. (C1.1.3)
  5. Warehouse submits the DR to Accounting to post General Ledger and Inventory entries. (C1.1.4.1)
  6. Driver collects the Delivery Receipts from Warehouse (may cover multiple clients on one route). (C1.1.4.2)
  7. Driver and Pahinante proceed to client locations.
  8. For pickup / no-delivery orders, Warehouse gives the DR to the customer for signature instead. (C1.1.4.3)
  9. On site, Pahinante weighs and records the cylinders, then delivers them. (C1.1.5.1)
  10. Client signs the DR, receives the items, and returns the empty cylinders. (C1.1.5.2)
  11. Check the 1-3kg residue threshold; round up or down and classify accordingly. (C1.1.5.3)
  12. Provide Credit Notes to the client, recording cylinder serial number and weight residue. (C1.1.6)
  13. Driver returns to the plant and weighs the empty cylinders. (C1.1.7)
  14. Driver submits delivery records plus the signed DR and AR to Accounting. (C1.1.8)
  15. Accounting reconciles the General Ledger against the signed DR and AR. (C1.1.9)
  16. Compute Commercial Gains - compare weigh-in vs weigh-out records from client site and plant.
  17. Record results in Google Sheets and ACCPAC.
  18. Compute commission and distribute to driver/helper/back-office team.

2Operational Guidelines

  • The residue classification threshold is fixed at 1-3kg; deviations are escalated to the Warehouse Supervisor, not decided ad hoc by the driver.
  • Two DRs and two Credit Notes are mandatory per delivery (client copy + company copy); a delivery without a matching document pair is not closed in the GL.
  • No cylinder leaves the warehouse without a Picklist reference; no GL/Inventory posting is made without a Warehouse-submitted DR.
  • Gains and commission are computed only from matched weigh-in/weigh-out pairs - unmatched records are held for investigation, never estimated.

4Current vs. Future Workflow

Current — Traditional / Manual
  • Paper DR/CR forms filled by hand at warehouse and client site.
  • GL reconciled manually against physical DR/AR copies.
  • Gains re-keyed twice - once to Google Sheets, once to ACCPAC.
  • Residue judged by the driver/Pahinante at point of delivery.
Future — Target / Automated
  • Odoo Sales Order auto-generates Picklist, DR and CR.
  • Digital client signature capture on delivery.
  • Automatic weigh-in/weigh-out variance flag and residue computation.
  • Single-entry GL posting, removing the GSheet/ACCPAC duplicate entry.

INDUSTRIAL (BULK)
Process Order | Delivery | Return to Plant
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Receive the booked bulk Sales Order; the order must fill the 8-ton truck capacity.
  2. Assign the lorry/trailer truck and load from the Storage Tank (stock transfer, tank to truck - no cylinders involved).
  3. Weigh the loaded truck at the plant truck scale (weigh-out).
  4. Generate loading documents: Receiving Report, Truck Scale slip, and DR - issued per lorry load, not per cylinder.
  5. Deliver the bulk LPG to the industrial or commercial client site.
  6. At the client site, measure the volume delivered via meter / pressure gauge.
  7. Client receives the bulk LPG and signs the DR.
  8. Truck returns to the plant and is weighed again (weigh-in).
  9. Compute Bulk Gains per truck - compare weigh-out vs weigh-in; withdrawal should read the higher figure.
  10. Record residue in the Commercial & Bulk LPG Residue Summary, per truck.
  11. Submit documents to Accounting; record in Google Sheets and ACCPAC.

2Operational Guidelines

  • Bulk orders must fill the 8-ton truck capacity before dispatch is scheduled; a partial load requires Delivery Head sign-off as a documented exception.
  • Bulk uses truck-scale (plant) + meter/pressure-gauge (client) measurement instead of cylinder weigh-in/out; both readings are logged before the trip is closed.
  • Per-truck gain is computed only once both weigh-out and weigh-in are on file - a truck missing either reading stays open, it is not estimated.

DEALER
Process Order | Plant Refilling & Swapping | Invoice
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Dealer client orders 2.5kg or 11kg cylinder variants and is scheduled in advance; only confirmed appointments are accommodated. (C3.1)
  2. Sales Rep creates a Sales Order Form noting the delivery/pickup date. (C3.1.2)
  3. Dealer arrives with empty cylinders; guard records the vehicle entry.
  4. Cylinder quantities are counted and the Entry/Exit Form (EEF/EES) is issued; guard validates tank classification (e.g. 11kg, sulit).
  5. Guard radios the Production Team; the checker collects the EEF.
  6. Cylinders are refilled at the refilling area (typically ~8 tons, about 2-3 hours).
  7. Checker encodes the transaction in AppSheet - this is the Refilling Report and the basis of the GRR (General Refilling Report).
  8. Entry Sheet is completed and the LUT is issued by the checker together with the delivery team.
  9. Joseph (Plant Operations Officer) reviews and confirms the transaction and records it in inventory.
  10. Accounting verifies via AppSheet against the official receipt.
  11. Checker verifies the receipt; quantity must match before release.
  12. Dealer proceeds to final inspection; guard performs final exit verification before release. (covers pickup-with-terms, pickup-with-payment, and delivery-with-terms variants, C3.1.2.1-C3.1.2.3)
  13. Cylinder Swapping (allied brands only - Taon Gas, Eco Cylinder, Sulit Gas): dealer submits a swap request via messaging; customer approval is secured.
  14. Cylinder photos are taken and sent to the designated group chat.
  15. Sir RY reviews and approves before the swap proceeds.
  16. Edmond (Warehouse and Quality Supervisor) handles the physical swap and keeps records; dealers must provide their own customer database.
  17. Operations keeps a monthly record of cylinders swapped, plus treatment and disposition.

2Operational Guidelines

  • Only confirmed, scheduled appointments are accommodated at the plant - walk-in dealer refills are not processed without a prior booking.
  • AppSheet entry and the official receipt must match in quantity before a transaction is released; a mismatch holds the release rather than being overridden at the gate.
  • Cylinder swapping is restricted to approved allied brands only and requires Sir RY's photo-based approval before any physical swap - no swap proceeds on verbal agreement alone.
  • Dealers must maintain their own customer database on file with Operations for swap traceability.

RETAIL
Process Order | Delivery/Pickup | Collect Payment
Partially confirmed — see gap note ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Retail/semi-dealer client orders 11kg or 2.5kg cylinders on a retail scale (smaller quantities than Dealer). (per source site: "same as Dealer steps")
  2. Order intake and refill/delivery fulfilment follow the same plant-refilling flow documented for Dealer, scaled to retail order volumes.
  3. Payment is collected via cash/COD at point of delivery or pickup rather than dealer credit terms - see Accounting > Retail for the cash-handling procedure.
  4. Commission/gains computation follows the same weigh-in/weigh-out and LUT-balancing logic used for Commercial and Dealer.

2Operational Guidelines

  • Retail fulfilment is explicitly modeled on the Dealer plant-refilling process per company specification; any retail-specific exception to that process is not yet documented.
  • Retail is COD by default; credit/terms sales are the exception and require the same past-dues check used in Accounting > Dealer before release.

COMPANY-WIDE
Purchase Order | Stock Transfer | Refill | Floor Stock
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Purchaser forwards the Purchase Order to the Refinery Supplier and schedules the inbound Lorry Truck delivery. (C5.1.1)
  2. Plant Manager receives the Lorry Trucks and collects the DR and Invoice from the delivery team.
  3. Plant Manager fills out the Receiving Report (RR) and unloads contents to the Storage Tank. (C5.1.2)
  4. Plant Staff measures the content volume using a gauge and documents the reading. (C5.1.3)
  5. Any quantity changes discovered after the RR are reported and logged as a separate entry. (C5.1.3.1)
  6. Stock Transfer: gas is refilled from the Storage Tank to cylinders following FIFO. (C5.1.4)
  7. Refilling is tracked using the Google Sheets/AppSheet tools already in use for cylinder refilling. (C5.1.5)
  8. Storage is split across two locations, Tank 1 and Tank 2; residue is tracked in two categories, Regular and WIWO, both affecting inventory and billing.
  9. Pull-out schedules and dates at client sites are tracked against a company Pull-out Calendar.
  10. Cylinders are purchased annually with individual serial numbers. (C5.3.1)
  11. Cylinders have a defined lifespan requiring repaint and re-testing, tracked by batch number. (C5.3.2)

2Operational Guidelines

  • Storage Tank stock transfer to cylinders must follow FIFO; no batch is filled out of sequence without Plant Manager sign-off.
  • Any quantity variance discovered after the Receiving Report is logged as a separate exception entry, never folded silently into the original RR figure.
  • Cylinder serials and batch numbers are tracked for their full lifespan (purchase through repaint/retest cycles); a cylinder without a traceable batch number does not re-enter circulation.
