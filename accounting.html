COMMERCIAL
Collection | Balance | Bank Recon
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Make the Invoice before delivery: check past receivables and dues with the customer. (A5C1.1)
  2. If the customer has past dues, flag the upcoming order. (A5C1.1.1)
  3. If past dues are within a passable / supervisor-approved range, approve the upcoming order. (A5C1.1.2)
  4. If there are no past dues, approve the upcoming order directly. (A5C1.1.3)
  5. Update and close Receivables: Acctg Staff collects the signed Delivery Receipt (DR) and Invoice from the Delivery Team. (A5C2.1.1)
  6. Check the monthly summary table of invoices for collection. (A5C2.1.2)
  7. Print the summary of Aged Receivables. (A5C2.1.3)
  8. Check and print the summary of Write-off / Loss Accounts. (A5C2.1.4)
  9. Bank Reconciliation: update and check the summary table of amounts for bank reconciliation. (A5C3.1)
  10. Resolve customer disputes: refer to the Sales Order; submit the paper to a superior if unresolved. (A5C4.1-A5C4.2)
  11. Payroll Incentive: prepare the incentive in payroll on the 15th and 30th. (A5C5.1)
  12. Prepare the Incentive template for Commercial and Semi-dealer using LUT forms (2.7kg, 11kg, 50kg) to balance empty and field cylinder data. (A5C5.2)

2Operational Guidelines

  • No invoice is issued before the past-dues check on the customer's AR; only a supervisor-approved past-due balance clears an order that has outstanding receivables.
  • Aged Receivables and Write-off/Loss summaries are printed monthly without exception, whether or not there's a specific collection issue that period.
  • Unresolved customer disputes are escalated by referring back to the original Sales Order and submitting to a superior - not settled informally at the collection desk.
  • Payroll incentives are computed only after LUT forms balance empty vs field cylinders; incentives are not paid out ahead of that balance check.

DEALER
Collection (Charge/Installment/PDC) | Balance | Bank Recon
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Before approving the order, verify the dealer's AR with CNC - check past dues and other factors first.
  2. Confirm the payment type: about 70% of dealers are on installment, about 30% are COD.
  3. For installment, collect Post-Dated Checks (PDCs) as payment security.
  4. If the dealer cannot issue PDCs, have them execute a Promissory Note instead.
  5. Track the installment schedule in Google Sheets (due dates and payment completion dates).
  6. Issue the Charge Invoice on terms and encode it in ACCPAC before sending to the client.
  7. File the countered hardcopy invoice; CNC accounts stay at CNC and a messenger brings them to the client.
  8. Update and close receivables; check the monthly summary of invoices for collection.
  9. Print the Aged Receivables summary and check write-off/loss accounts.
  10. Perform bank reconciliation and resolve dealer disputes (refer back to the Sales Order).
  11. Apply dealer pricing from the MGC Pricing Matrix (per-channel buffer and per-dealer adjustment).

2Operational Guidelines

  • Dealer AR is checked against CNC before any order is approved - this runs even for dealers presumed current, not only for flagged accounts.
  • A dealer without PDCs is not simply denied credit - a Promissory Note is the required fallback before COD is enforced.
  • Installment schedules are tracked in a dedicated Google Sheet with due dates and completion dates; a missed due date should surface there, not only get noticed at the monthly Aged Receivables review.
  • CNC-held accounts are physically couriered by messenger, matching the CNC filing convention already in place.

SEMI-DEALER
Collection (Cash/COD) | Balance | Bank Recon
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Issue the entry/exit slip (EES) to the customer, encode it, and have the guard validate the tank classification.
  2. After loading, the checker goes to Accounting; issue the cashier a cash/charge invoice or DR.
  3. Cashier collects the cash; the receipt (recibo) must match the empty and filled cylinders (the LUT contents).
  4. Checker encodes in AppSheet (= Refilling Report); AppSheet and the receipt must match (basis of the GRR).
  5. Forward Semi-dealer cash invoices straight to the cashier at Plant Accounting; collect cash and documents by 4-5pm.
  6. Balance sales and the LUT within the same day (2.7kg, 11kg, 50kg must be indicated and balance).
  7. Audit cash sales, collections, and returns from advances.
  8. Validate the cash collection report from the cashier and check the deposit balancing at the bank.
  9. Prepare the payroll incentive (15th and 30th) using LUT forms to balance empty and field cylinders.

2Operational Guidelines

  • The recibo must match empty and filled cylinder counts (LUT contents) before cash is considered collected - a mismatch holds the transaction rather than being resolved by adjusting the receipt after the fact.
  • AppSheet entry and the physical receipt must match in quantity, mirroring the same rule used in Operations > Dealer refilling.
  • Sales and the LUT are balanced same-day, not at month-end - this is a daily close, not a periodic one.
  • Cash and documents are due to the Plant Accounting cashier by 4-5pm; a late submission is an exception to flag, not a routine occurrence.

OVERALL
Monitoring | Updates | Reports
Confirmed with source data ▾

1SOP Manual — Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Consolidate and check all relevant data on request - bi-monthly or monthly Financial Statements. (A5A1)
  2. Produce versatile, multi-format financial statements and payroll summary; drill into individual summary amounts. (A5A1.1-A5A1.2)
  3. Generate instant PDF or Excel exports for further analysis; separate data by channel; compare Budget vs Actual. (A5A1.3-A5A1.5)
  4. Profit and Loss Statement: ensure all entries are encoded, refresh the P&L table, and print the monthly P&L Statement. (A5C5.1.1)
  5. Cash Flow Statement: ensure all entries are encoded, refresh the Cash Flow table, and print the monthly CF Statement. (A5C5.1.2)
  6. Balance Sheet: ensure all entries are encoded, refresh the Balance Sheet table, and print the monthly BS Statement. (A5C5.1.3)
  7. Prepare the Payroll Summary, consolidating all incentive computations and salary data for the period. (A5C5.2)
  8. Industrial Projects: track Budget P&L against Actual P&L and timeline. (A5C5.3)

2Operational Guidelines

  • All three financial statements (P&L, Cash Flow, Balance Sheet) require full entry encoding before the monthly refresh - a partial encode is not printed as final.
  • Industrial Projects are tracked with a Budget P&L set at project start, compared against Actual P&L throughout - variance review is continuous, not just at project close.
  • The Payroll Summary is only finalized after the Incentive template (LUT-balanced, see Commercial/Semi-dealer) is complete for the period.

CROSS-STAGE GOVERNANCE
Escalation and audit-trail rules that apply on top of every Accounting approval row above.
▾

2Operational Guidelines

  • Any transaction blocked or disputed at its normal approval level escalates through the standard chain (System watch -> Review -> Manager) rather than being resolved informally between the two parties involved.
  • Sales Order Control Numbers, once assigned, are permanent and non-editable; a correction is made with a new document, never by editing the original control number.
  • The person who initiates a transaction is not the same person who posts it to the GL or reconciles it - segregation of duties applies at every posting step.
